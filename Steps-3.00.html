Data Overview 3

Data Sources and KPIs

The US Regional Sales dataset, spanning from 2018 to 2020, was gathered from retail sales records through Microsoft SQL Server, covering transactions across multiple sales channels including In-Store, Online, Distributor, and Wholesale. This dataset is available on Kaggle, a reputable online data repository. It is structured to enable the analysis of sales performance, resource allocation, and pricing strategies for optimizing sales channel performance and maximizing profitability. 



System Architecture
The architecture focuses on optimizing sales and profitability by analyzing market trends and inventory efficiency. It involves gathering historical sales data, preprocessing and cleansing it, selecting key performance metrics, and applying data modeling techniques using Python. Predictive models are built using Spark for scalable data processing, and insights are visualized in Power BI to drive strategic decision-making. The project integrates Microsoft Fabric with Power BI for data visualization and reporting, enabling enhanced decision-making and operational efficiency across all sales channels.


Data Features
The dataset includes key variables such as Order Number, Sales Channel, Warehouse Code, Procured Date, Order Date, Ship Date, Delivery Date, Sales Team ID, Customer ID, Store ID, Product ID, Order Quantity, Discount Applied, Unit Cost, and Unit Price. These features are essential for evaluating sales performance, identifying product trends, and optimizing sales strategies. The data supports decision-making processes by enabling the prediction of demand patterns and sales channel performance, improving profitability, and enhancing operational efficiency across regions.
